Making an HTML5 Game

Mar 2, 2018

Design Decisions

What am I doing?

I'm making a pure javascript HTML5 game from scratch, rendered on <canvas>. I've always wanted to make a game, so once I got inspired by Ballz on iOS, I decided to make it. I love idle games and I think an idle game with just a little bit of skill could be interesting. Therefore, a game where you send balls out to bounce around to break a bunch of blocks sounds like a nice compromise between idling and doing.

Why am I blogging this?

I'm practicing making blogs and explaining my thought process.

Why from scratch?

The two tools that should be considered is a physics engine, a game engine, and/or an animation engine. First and foremost, I don't want to rummage through the different types of engines are research or try any of them. I want to get started right away. I believe the game is about on the level of cookie clicker which (I believe) was made from scratch, so I believe making my game in the same way is reasonable. I think that learning to implement the physics, rendering, and game loop myself will be useful for future projects.
